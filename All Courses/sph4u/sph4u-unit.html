1. Scientific Investigation Skills and Career Exploration ▼
  • Plan, perform, and record scientific investigations (inquiry & research).
  • Analyze and communicate results clearly using qualitative & quantitative methods.
  • Explore physics-related careers and contributions of scientists, including Canadians.
  • Time: 10 Hours
2. Dynamics ▼
  • Investigate forces in uniform circular motion and motion in a plane.
  • Solve qualitative and quantitative motion problems.
  • Analyze technologies that apply dynamics and assess social/environmental impact.
  • Time: 20 Hours
3. Energy and Momentum ▼
  • Examine work, energy, momentum, and conservation laws (1D & 2D).
  • Use labs/simulations to model collisions and energy transfer.
  • Evaluate technologies/procedures using energy & momentum principles.
  • Time: 20 Hours
4. Gravitational, Electric, and Magnetic Fields ▼
  • Investigate field concepts, properties, and laws; solve field problems.
  • Describe interactions of fields with matter.
  • Analyze field‑based technologies and their social/environmental impact.
  • Time: 20 Hours
5. The Wave Nature of Light ▼
  • Study wave and light behavior: diffraction, refraction, interference, polarization.
  • Solve quantitative problems using wave models.
  • Assess technologies that use the wave nature of light.
  • Time: 20 Hours
6. Revolutions in Modern Physics: Quantum Mechanics and Special Relativity ▼
  • Investigate core concepts of quantum mechanics and special relativity.
  • Interpret evidence supporting these models and solve related problems.
  • Discuss how new theories shift scientific thought and enable new tech.
  • Time: 15 Hours
Review, Summative and Exam ▼
  • Comprehensive review of all strands and final evaluation tasks.
  • Time: 5 Hours
Evaluation Breakdown ▼
  • 70% – Term Work (products, observations, conversations)
  • 30% – Final Evaluation (summative + exam)
  • Category Weights: Knowledge & Understanding – 25%, Thinking – 25%, Communication – 25%, Application – 25%
Teaching & Learning Strategies ▼
  • Direct instruction, teacher‑facilitated discussions, and small‑group work.
  • Labs/experiential learning, independent practice, worksheets & problem solving.
  • Use of technology (simulations, computer/Internet), videos, and presentations.
  • Brainstorming, conferencing/feedback, and inquiry‑based investigations.
Resources ▼
  • Text: Physics 12 (Nelson, 2007)
  • Other: Various internet resources
  • Reference: No required textbook; PowerPoints, notes, and solved examples are provided and required for learning.

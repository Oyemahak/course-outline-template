1. Building Reading Skills ▼
  • Read and respond to informational, narrative, and graphic texts.
  • Understand organization and features of reports, stories, textbooks, news/magazine articles, and short fiction.
  • Apply before/during/after reading strategies to interpret content and meaning.
  • Use strategies to understand unfamiliar and specialized vocabulary.
  • Time: 35 Hours
2. Building Writing Skills ▼
  • Use the writing process to plan, draft, revise, and produce polished pieces.
  • Write summaries, information paragraphs, opinion pieces (series of paragraphs), and news reports.
  • Match form to audience and purpose; include graphic elements when appropriate.
  • Time: 35 Hours
3. Understanding and Assessing Growth in Literacy ▼
  • Recognize the importance of communication skills at school, work, and home.
  • Understand roles and responsibilities in the learning process.
  • Explain reading and writing processes and their role in learning.
  • Assess and reflect on personal growth in literacy during the course.
  • Time: 35 Hours
Review, Summative & Exam ▼
  • Course review and culminating evaluations demonstrating overall literacy skills.
  • Time: 5 Hours
Eligibility Requirement ▼
  • Students eligible to write the OSSLT at least twice and unsuccessful at least once may take this course.
  • Students who have already met the literacy requirement may take the course under special circumstances at the principal’s discretion.
Evaluation Breakdown ▼
  • 70% – Term Work (products, observations, conversations)
  • 30% – Final Evaluation (summative + exam)
  • Categories: Knowledge & Understanding – 20%, Thinking & Inquiry – 20%, Application – 40%, Communication – 20%
Teaching & Learning Strategies ▼
  • Direct instruction; teacher‑facilitated class and small‑group discussions.
  • Worksheets/surveys, independent work, brainstorming, presentations.
  • Use of computers/Internet, video/audio materials, experiential learning.
  • Conferencing, peer/self assessment, inquiry‑based activities.
Resources ▼
  • Core: Literature and Media – Nelson English.
  • Additional: A variety of news articles, videos, and books.

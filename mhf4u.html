📘 MHF4U – Grade 12 Advanced Functions

🗓️ Units and Timeline

Unit Title Expectations Summary Time
1 Exponential and Logarithmic Functions Understand exponential-log relationships, laws of logs, graphs, and equations for real-world problems. 25 hrs
2 Trigonometric Functions Explore radian measure, graphs, identities, reciprocal functions, and trigonometric equations. 30 hrs
3 Polynomial and Rational Functions Connect numeric, algebraic, graphical models, solve equations and inequalities. 25 hrs
4 Characteristics of Functions Compare functions, calculate rate of change, perform operations and compositions. 25 hrs
Final Exam and Summative Final evaluation of all learning. 5 hrs

🧠 What You Will Learn – Curriculum Expectations

  • Understand relationships between exponential and logarithmic expressions
  • Apply logarithmic laws to simplify and evaluate expressions
  • Graph and interpret logarithmic functions
  • Solve exponential and logarithmic equations including real-life applications
  • Use radian measure to describe angles and arcs
  • Graph primary and reciprocal trigonometric functions
  • Prove trigonometric identities
  • Solve trigonometric equations with real applications
  • Analyze and graph polynomial functions of various degrees
  • Understand rational functions and asymptotic behavior
  • Solve rational equations and inequalities algebraically and graphically
  • Determine average and instantaneous rate of change
  • Perform operations and compositions of functions
  • Compare properties of different types of functions
  • Model and solve complex problems with function behavior

📊 Evaluation Breakdown

70% – Term Work (assignments, quizzes, unit tests, projects)

30% – Final Evaluation (summative + exam)

  • 🧠 Knowledge & Understanding – 20%
  • 💡 Thinking & Inquiry – 20%
  • 🛠 Application – 40%

🎓 Teaching & Learning Strategies

Students learn using a mix of:

  • ✔ Direct Instruction and teacher-facilitated discussions
  • ✔ Worksheets, brainstorming, and problem-solving tasks
  • ✔ Independent and group-based learning
  • ✔ Real-world applications using technology and visualizations

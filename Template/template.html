📚 Course Description

This course extends students’ experience with functions. Students will investigate the properties of polynomial, rational, logarithmic, and trigonometric functions; develop techniques for combining functions; broaden their understanding of rates of change; and develop facility in applying these concepts and skills. This course is intended both for students taking the Calculus and Vectors course as a prerequisite for a university program and for those wishing to consolidate their understanding of mathematics before proceeding to any one of a variety of university programs.

🗓️ Units and Timeline

Unit Title Expectations Summary Time
1 Exponential and Logarithmic Functions Understand exponential-log relationships, laws of logs, graphs, and equations for real-world problems. 25 Hours
2 Trigonometric Functions Explore radian measure, graphs, identities, reciprocal functions, and trigonometric equations. 30 Hours
3 Polynomial and Rational Functions Connect numeric, algebraic, graphical models, solve equations and inequalities. 25 Hours
4 Characteristics of Functions Compare functions, calculate rate of change, perform operations and compositions. 25 Hours
Final Exam and Summative Final evaluation of all learning. 5 Hours

🧠 What You Will Learn – Curriculum Expectations

  • Understand relationships between exponential and logarithmic expressions
  • Apply logarithmic laws to simplify and evaluate expressions
  • Graph and interpret logarithmic functions
  • Solve exponential and logarithmic equations including real-life applications
  • Use radian measure to describe angles and arcs
  • Graph primary and reciprocal trigonometric functions
  • Prove trigonometric identities
  • Solve trigonometric equations with real applications
  • Analyze and graph polynomial functions of various degrees
  • Understand rational functions and asymptotic behavior
  • Solve rational equations and inequalities algebraically and graphically
  • Determine average and instantaneous rate of change
  • Perform operations and compositions of functions
  • Compare properties of different types of functions
  • Model and solve complex problems with function behavior
